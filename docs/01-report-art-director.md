# Team Report: Art Director

**Role:** Creative Direction, Visual Concept, UX Strategy

## Scope of Work

Overarching creative vision for the Ukho Archive — conceptual framework, visual language, user experience strategy. Led all high-level aesthetic and structural decisions, defined the analog-digital design philosophy, and provided ongoing creative direction across design, development, and media preparation.

## Deliverables

### 1. Creative Concept

Development of the archive's core identity: a digital platform treating 14 years of curatorial work as living institutional memory rather than a static catalogue. Definition of the tension between archival formality and experimental digital expression as the project's visual thesis. Conceptualization of the CRT/analog aesthetic as reference to the materiality of live performance — impermanent, textured, slightly unstable.

### 2. Visual Language Direction

Selection and approval of the typographic system: Archaism as display face (connecting to the archival theme), Satoshi as the functional layer (contemporary, neutral), Commit Mono as the data/system voice (terminal aesthetic). Definition of the color strategy: restrained black opacity hierarchy for content, accent green (#4af626) as the only saturated color (referencing analog signal indicators, CRT phosphor), blue (#0000ff) for navigation (raw, undesigned, hyperlink-native).

### 3. UX Strategy

Defined five navigation paradigms and the rationale for each: Card Index for visual browsing (poster-forward, spatial), List for data-oriented research (searchable, comprehensive), Everything for entity-based discovery (performers, pieces, venues as entry points), Riddles for curated serendipity (non-linear, playful), Portals for future experimental formats. Established the principle that each mode offers a fundamentally different relationship to the same 180 events.

### 4. Animation & Interaction Philosophy

Direction on the role of motion: animations should feel systemic rather than decorative — boot sequences reference the archive "loading" its memory, card cascades suggest physical filing, the CRT overlay implies content being transmitted/projected. Defined the interactive dice as a navigation element introducing chance — a curatorial gesture embedded in the interface. Approved all timing, easing, and choreography decisions.

### 5. CRT/Analog Aesthetic Direction

Specification of the analog overlay as persistent environmental layer rather than filter: grain density calibrated to be felt but not read, scanlines as rhythm rather than ornament, glitch as controlled instability (low probability, high impact), vignette as spatial framing. Direction that effects degrade gracefully — visible on capable hardware, invisible on low-end devices.

### 6. Art Direction of Media Assets

Guidelines for poster presentation (original proportions preserved, no cropping, white background integration), photography selection criteria (documentary over promotional, performance moments over posed portraits), overall media tone (authentic, unpolished, institutional).

### 7. Design Reviews & Iteration

Ongoing review of all design and development output. Feedback on typography sizing, color opacity values, spacing, animation timing. Final approval on each page and interaction. Direction on refinements — menu transparency, filter tab treatment, dice scale, font weight, element alignment.
